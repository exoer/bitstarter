Are you the kind of person who bookmarks a lot of web-pages? How much do you remember what you bookmarked a month ago? What do you think you could accomplish if you remembered more of the stuff that you bookmarked?

The problem with bookmarking is that the information stays on your computer, not in your head. But now we have a great thing for you. MemoButton is a new tool that is designed to help you remember more of your surfing. It is a button on your toolbar that captures your discoveries and turn them into quiz cards (with no tedious typing!). MemoButton ensures that you get return on your investment of learning, by quizzing you at the best times for remembering. Because, as you know, we forget what we don't repeat. In this way you will keep growing your active knowledge and stay on top of the game.

Memobutton is an extension to Google Chrome. It helps you capture snippets of information and turn them into quizz-cards with no typing.

To make sure you remember the stuff that are important to you, MemoButton uses a spaced repetition learning algorithm so that you can review the quiz-cards at the best time for remembering.

FAQ

What is spaced repetition learning
Spaced repetition learning is a technique where you review your learning at specific times. For example after 2 days, then after 6 days and depending on how well you know the material there is an increasing interval. The benefit is that your knowledge will stay active in your memory.
How about other browsers?
We plan to support Firefox and Safari if this Crowd funding campaign is successful.
